Vision

A long-term national direction for moving from managed decline to generational stewardship.

What If Australia Chose A Different Path?

What if Australia planned for the next 25 to 50 years, instead of governing around three-year election cycles?

What if government focused on the things only government should do well: infrastructure, essential services, national capability, shared systems, border integrity, public safety and long-term stewardship?

What if we moved away from social engineering, market distortion and managed decline, and returned government to its proper role: protecting citizens, building prosperity and leaving the country stronger than we found it?

What if the tax system was simpler, flatter and fairer?

What if Australia stopped treating working citizens as the resource, and started taxing the nation's natural resources properly?

What if we shifted from wealth redistribution to wealth creation?

What if aspiration was supported, not punished?

What if we taxed more of what we consume, and less of what we earn?

What if the hidden taxes that drive up fuel, housing, transport, business costs and household bills were removed?

What if multinational companies paid a fairer share, and Australia stopped giving away strategic resources for pennies on the dollar?

What if policy focused first on Australian citizens: their wages, homes, families, services, security and future?

What if we worked toward a common national goal, built a sovereign wealth base, and left our children and their children a stronger country?

The Australian Prosperity Program asks what Australia could become if we changed how we think about government, tax, equality, resources and wealth.

It is a long-term reform model built around a simple idea: Australia should be governed as an inheritance to be strengthened, not a budget cycle to be managed.

The Direction

The model is not built around one election cycle.

It asks what Australia could achieve over 25 to 50 years if government focused on stewardship, infrastructure, shared services, citizen prosperity, resource wealth and national capability.

It starts from a simple premise: a resource-rich country should not leave its citizens overtaxed, underhoused, energy-poor and dependent on short-term political fixes.

The goal is to move from managed decline to generational stewardship.

What This Means

The Australian Prosperity Program argues for:

  • long-term planning over short election-cycle management;
  • wealth creation over wealth redistribution;
  • simpler, flatter and more transparent taxation;
  • lower taxes on work and enterprise;
  • stronger taxation of national resources where economic rent is being extracted;
  • government focused on infrastructure, essential services, national capability and public safety;
  • reduced market distortion, social engineering and bureaucratic duplication;
  • a Future Generations Fund that turns national wealth into long-term national strength;
  • a citizen-first approach to public policy.
