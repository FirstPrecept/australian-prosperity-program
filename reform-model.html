The Reform Model

How the Australian Prosperity Program shifts Australia from tax and distribution toward national wealth creation.

From Tax And Distribution To National Wealth Creation

The Australian Prosperity Program (APP) starts from a simple diagnosis.

Australia has become too dependent on taxing current income, running complex transfer systems and using temporary budget measures to manage long-term pressure.

That model is hard to sustain.

The reform program proposes a different model:

  1. reset the tax base;
  2. discipline spending;
  3. capture fair value from finite resources;
  4. convert part of that value into permanent national assets;
  5. use long-term wealth to reduce future tax pressure and strengthen essential services.

This is a national wealth creation model.

The Civic Purpose

Many Australians are disengaged from politics and governance because they do not see a serious long-term plan.

They see more of the same: higher costs, more complexity, more taxes, more pressure and no clear end point.

The reform model attempts to give the country a shared purpose: steward national wealth, rebuild prosperity, lower structural cost pressures and create a better future.

It also makes clear that there should be an end in sight: an end to the current tax-and-cost spiral, permanent short-term crisis management and treating Australians as an unlimited revenue source.

First-Principles Government

Government should exist to do the things citizens cannot do alone: protect the nation, uphold law and order, build and maintain infrastructure, provide shared services, steward national wealth, protect basic rights and equal citizenship, provide essential services and a genuine safety net, and build long-term national capability.

Government should be a trusted steward serving all Australians — protecting citizens, building shared prosperity, and never treating people as subjects to manage or resources to mine.

Government should not exist for social engineering, controlling every part of people's lives, endless bureaucracy or using Australians as an unlimited revenue source.

The Nine Foundation Pillars

  • Revenue Reform
  • Spending Discipline
  • National Wealth Creation
  • Economic Growth
  • Cost of Living Relief
  • Equal Citizenship
  • Aspiration and Reward
  • Simplicity and Reset
  • Intergenerational Stewardship

These pillars are designed to work together.

Tax reform without spending discipline is unstable. Resource reform without a Future Generations Fund can be consumed by annual budgets. Cost-of-living relief without structural reform becomes another temporary handout.

Tax Reset

  • $40,000 tax-free threshold;
  • 25% personal income tax above the threshold;
  • 25% company tax;
  • broad 20% GST;
  • no negative gearing;
  • no CGT discount;
  • simplified deductions, offsets and concessions.

The goal is not just lower tax. The goal is a simpler system that is easier to understand, harder to game and better aligned with work, saving, enterprise and investment.

Spending Discipline

The APP model supports a humane safety net and strong essential services.

But it rejects permanent redistribution as the central purpose of government.

The safety net should protect Australians in genuine hardship. It should not become a permanent lifestyle system or a tool for endless redistribution.

Spending discipline means essential functions first, wealth creation next, safety net support for genuine need, careful treatment of discretionary programs, clear budget rules and transparent reporting.

Resource Wealth Reform

Resource Wealth Reform is the mechanism for capturing fair public value from finite resources.

The current numerical model is gas-focused.

  • gas royalty floor / rent capture;
  • PRRT repair / netback pricing;
  • valuation integrity;
  • BEPS and transfer-pricing controls;
  • project-level reporting;
  • 50% net gas resource-rent allocation to the Future Generations Fund.
Contribution treatment The 50% allocation is part of the $75B mature contribution target. It is not added on top of it. Broader resources are not yet numerically modelled.

Future Generations Fund

The Future Generations Fund is the institutional expression of the resource-to-wealth strategy.

Resource Wealth Reform captures fair value. The Future Generations Fund preserves and invests a defined share of that value.

Norway proves the principle. APP adapts the model.

Structural Cost-Of-Living Relief

The APP model does not rely on temporary cost-of-living handouts alone.

  • lower and simpler income tax;
  • housing supply and housing security reform;
  • energy affordability and sovereignty;
  • infrastructure that lowers hidden costs;
  • government simplification;
  • lower compliance burden;
  • long-term tax-pressure reduction from national wealth.

Some of these effects are not yet numerically modelled. They should be treated as qualitative benefits until assumptions are approved.

Core Civic Doctrine

Equal citizenship

Equal citizenship means the same rules, same standing, same treatment and same basic services. It does not mean equal incomes, equal outcomes or unlimited taxpayer-funded benefits.

Needs-blind essentials

Basic services should be provided according to need, not wealth or status. But not every payment, medicine, service or support is essential.

Aspiration and reward

Australia should reward people who work, save, build skills, start businesses, take risks and improve their lives.

A Model For Testing

This is a discussion model. It needs scrutiny. It needs independent testing. It needs better annual ramp modelling.

But it offers a serious alternative to a system that keeps asking current taxpayers to carry more pressure without building enough permanent national wealth.
