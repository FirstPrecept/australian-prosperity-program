Contact And Engagement

A neutral engagement page for serious review, testing and critique of the Australian Prosperity Program.

Purpose

The Australian Prosperity Program is a policy development project for serious discussion.

This page is a neutral engagement page for people and organisations interested in reviewing the Australian Prosperity Program (APP).

The reform model is open for serious discussion, testing and critique.

Who This Is For

  • political parties;
  • independents;
  • parliamentary advisers;
  • policy committees;
  • civic organisations;
  • researchers;
  • economists;
  • public finance specialists;
  • interested citizens.

What We Are Seeking

We are seeking serious feedback on the reform model, 25-year wealth creation pathway, tax transition design, spending discipline, Resource Wealth Reform, Future Generations Fund design, NDIS sustainability and boundary reform, government efficiency assumptions, implementation sequencing and public communication.

Discussion Principles

  • serious;
  • evidence-based;
  • plain-English;
  • open to criticism;
  • honest about assumptions;
  • focused on long-term national interest.

What This Is Not

Clear boundary This is not a final election-costed platform. It is not a promise that every number is settled. It is not a short-term promotional brochure. It is a reform model for testing.

Useful Starting Questions

  • Is the national wealth creation model worth developing further?
  • Are the tax reset settings workable?
  • Are the spending discipline assumptions realistic?
  • Is the Future Generations Fund contribution pathway credible?
  • How should the gas-focused Resource Wealth Reform model be validated?
  • What would a fully ramped annual cashflow model need to include?
  • Which parts of the implementation roadmap are strongest?
  • Which parts need redesign?

Engagement Pathway

  1. Review the website summary.
  2. Read the discussion pack.
  3. Identify assumptions that need testing.
  4. Provide written feedback.
  5. Request a focused briefing if there is serious interest.
  6. Commission or support independent modelling where appropriate.

Feedback channel coming soon. In the meantime, this site is being prepared for structured review by interested policy stakeholders.

Planned Feedback Form

A structured feedback form is being prepared to capture whether reviewers are positive, mixed, negative or unsure; whether the model deserves further development; which policy areas are strongest; which areas need the most work; and whether reviewers are willing to provide follow-up feedback.

Core Statement

Australia should not be limited to managing decline through higher taxes and more transfers.

Build national wealth, protect essential services, reward effort and leave future Australians with stronger national assets.
