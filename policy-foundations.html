Policy Foundations

Structured policy cards for the pillars that support the reform model.

Structured Policy Cards

APP means the Australian Prosperity Program - a reform model and policy development project.

APP is not a launched political party. This material is a discussion model, not a final election-costed platform.

These cards summarise the current active Volume II policy chapters in plain English. They do not replace full costing, legal review, implementation design or public testing.

Key caveats
  • The $75B Future Generations Fund figure is a mature base-case target, not a Year 1 amount.
  • The model is not yet a fully ramped annual cashflow model.
  • NDIS, government efficiency and gas revenue assumptions need validation.
  • Non-gas resources are not yet numerically modelled.

APP Reform Program

Aim

  • Move Australia from a revenue-and-distribution model to a revenue, distribution and wealth creation model.
  • Build permanent national assets so future income does not rely only on current taxpayers.

New Measures

  • Adopt the allocation order: wealth creation first, essential government functions second, discretionary programs third.
  • Treat investment returns as a fourth long-term source of national income alongside tax, resource revenue and growth.

Changes To Existing Systems

  • Align tax, spending, resource, housing, energy and infrastructure policy around national wealth creation.
  • Shift budget thinking away from consuming all current revenue each year.

Abolished Or Phased Out

  • No major abolition proposed in this summary.

Why It Matters

  • It gives the whole reform model a common purpose: lower future tax pressure, stronger public finances and a better inheritance for future Australians.

Fiscal Framework

Aim

  • Make public finances sustainable, disciplined and focused on stewardship.
  • Protect essential services without making permanent redistribution the central purpose of government.

New Measures

  • Introduce a structural surplus rule in normal economic conditions.
  • Automatically transfer structural surpluses to the Future Generations Fund.
  • Require major legislation to consider future generations, national wealth and resilience.

Changes To Existing Systems

  • Limit deficits to recession, war, national emergency or severe natural disaster.
  • Limit debt to national emergencies, nation-building infrastructure and defence mobilisation.
  • Apply 10-year sunset reviews to programs, agencies and regulations.

Abolished Or Phased Out

  • Phase down Tier 3 discretionary spending first during fiscal pressure.
  • Stop using debt for recurring spending or political promises.

Why It Matters

  • Fiscal discipline prevents tax reform and resource gains from being swallowed by uncontrolled spending.

Tax Reform

Aim

  • Create a simpler, fairer and more growth-focused tax system.
  • Reward work, saving, investment and enterprise while funding essential services.

New Measures

  • Set a $40,000 tax-free threshold.
  • Apply a 25% personal income tax rate above the threshold.
  • Apply a 25% company tax rate.
  • Apply a broad 20% GST.
  • Strengthen anti-avoidance rules for trusts, income splitting, related-party transactions, offshore profit shifting, transfer pricing and beneficial ownership.

Changes To Existing Systems

  • Keep franking credits to avoid double taxation of company profits.
  • Replace complex deductions, offsets and concessions with simpler rules unless a clear public purpose exists.
  • Treat further tax reductions and payroll tax transition as long-term goals only when fiscally sustainable.

Abolished Or Phased Out

  • Remove negative gearing for future investment purchases, subject to grandfathering.
  • Remove the CGT discount and replace it with a simpler, more neutral framework.
  • Phase out the deduction maze where deductions do not serve a clear public purpose.

Why It Matters

  • It lowers complexity, rewards productive effort and gives the reform model a clearer revenue base.

Future Generations Fund

Aim

  • Convert temporary resource wealth, structural surpluses and selected extraordinary receipts into permanent national assets.
  • Adapt the Norwegian sovereign wealth principle to Australian conditions.

New Measures

  • Create an independently governed fund with a long-term investment mandate.
  • Transfer at least 50% of net resource rent revenues to the Fund.
  • Transfer structural surpluses to the Fund after essential functions and prudential buffers are met.
  • Target a long-term Fund value of $3 trillion to $5 trillion over about 25 years.

Changes To Existing Systems

  • Protect capital withdrawals during the first 25 years except for extreme national emergencies.
  • After Year 25, allow ordinary distributions of up to 3% of rolling average Fund value, subject to capital preservation.
  • Prioritise distributions toward tax reduction, childcare, education and future national priorities.

Abolished Or Phased Out

  • Prevent short-term political raids on the Fund.
  • Stop treating resource windfalls as ordinary money for annual spending.

Why It Matters

  • The $75B mature base-case contribution target is not Year 1. The Fund is central to lowering future tax pressure and protecting national wealth over time.

Resource Wealth Reform

Aim

  • Capture fair public value from finite national resources.
  • Turn part of that value into permanent national wealth instead of consuming it through annual budgets.

New Measures

  • Build a resource wealth system using valuation integrity, minimum public return, profit-based rent capture, project ring-fencing, public reporting and Future Generations Fund allocation.
  • Repair PRRT integrity and use netback pricing where it improves valuation.
  • Apply BEPS controls to related-party debt, offshore service charges, marketing fees, insurance, shipping and other profit-shifting channels.

Changes To Existing Systems

  • Use baseline royalties or equivalent mechanisms to ensure a minimum public return.
  • Require project-level reporting and beneficial ownership transparency.
  • Transfer at least 50% of net resource rent revenue to the Future Generations Fund.

Abolished Or Phased Out

  • Remove artificial sheltering of profitable projects through unrelated losses or non-commercial deductions.
  • Stop double counting the 50% Fund allocation; it is part of the mature $75B base-case target, not an extra amount.

Why It Matters

  • The current numerical model is gas-focused. Gas revenue assumptions need validation and non-gas resources are not yet numerically modelled.

Housing

Aim

  • Restore attainable ownership, improve affordability and guarantee housing security for Australians who genuinely need help.
  • Treat housing security as a social foundation while keeping home ownership as a national objective.

New Measures

  • Establish the Housing Security Scheme for eligible citizens who cannot reasonably house themselves.
  • Keep government ownership, provide secure long-term tenure, charge no rent and return the property to public ownership on death or relinquishment.
  • Directly purchase required social housing stock from developers where governments require allocations.

Changes To Existing Systems

  • Expand housing security stock through predictable construction and acquisition.
  • Speed up approvals, reduce duplication and link housing growth to infrastructure.
  • Gradually replace stamp duty with a modest land-based framework.

Abolished Or Phased Out

  • Prohibit foreign ownership of established residential housing.
  • Remove sale, mortgage and leverage rights from Housing Security Scheme properties.

Why It Matters

  • Housing reform lowers cost pressure, improves workforce mobility and supports family formation.

Family, Population And Migration

Aim

  • Build a prosperous, cohesive and sustainable nation that can renew itself across generations.
  • Link population growth to housing, infrastructure, workforce needs and national interest.

New Measures

  • Support family formation through housing affordability, lower taxes, lower living costs, childcare, security and confidence.
  • Use skills-based, selective, values-compatible and infrastructure-linked migration.
  • Apply a contribution-first principle for most non-essential welfare.

Changes To Existing Systems

  • Review population settings against housing, infrastructure, healthcare, education and labour market capacity.
  • Focus migration on genuine shortages instead of wage suppression or replacing domestic workforce development.
  • Keep citizenship meaningful through civic understanding and respect for Australian institutions.

Abolished Or Phased Out

  • Phase out population growth as an automatic objective.
  • Restrict long-term non-essential welfare where contribution and citizenship standards are not met.

Why It Matters

  • It stops growth from permanently outrunning services, housing and infrastructure.

Education

Aim

  • Build capable citizens, skilled workers, independent thinkers and future leaders.
  • Give every child a fair baseline in literacy, numeracy, critical thinking, civic understanding and useful skills.

New Measures

  • Put critical thinking first, followed by trades, academic excellence, personal development, civic education and employment readiness.
  • Give trades and vocational pathways equal status with university pathways.
  • Support apprenticeship support parity.

Changes To Existing Systems

  • Align public funding with capability development and national needs.
  • Favour priority disciplines such as medicine, engineering, science, technology, agriculture and teaching.
  • Make education and training pathways easier for students, parents, apprentices, employers and institutions to understand.

Abolished Or Phased Out

  • No major abolition proposed in this summary.

Why It Matters

  • Education builds the workforce needed for productivity, housing, infrastructure, healthcare, energy and defence capability.

Energy

Aim

  • Deliver reliable, affordable and secure energy.
  • End the problem of Australia being energy-rich while households and businesses are energy-poor.

New Measures

  • Apply the hierarchy: reliability, energy independence, affordability, environmental stewardship and speed of deployment.
  • Support staged nuclear power as long-term baseload generation.
  • Establish domestic gas reservation and strategic fuel reserves for diesel, petrol and aviation fuel.

Changes To Existing Systems

  • Strengthen the grid through upgrades, storage, redundancy and security.
  • Keep a diversified generation mix instead of relying on one technology.
  • Use energy policy to support manufacturing, processing, critical minerals and heavy industry.

Abolished Or Phased Out

  • No major abolition proposed in this summary.

Why It Matters

  • Lower and more reliable energy reduces household pressure and improves national competitiveness.

Infrastructure

Aim

  • Treat infrastructure as an investment in productivity, resilience, housing supply and national capability.
  • Lower hidden costs across the economy.

New Measures

  • Complete a national freight rail corridor linking regional Australia, capital cities, industrial centres, ports and Western Sydney Airport.
  • Build housing-enabling infrastructure before growth overwhelms services.
  • Develop economic corridors linking industry, housing, freight, energy and regions.

Changes To Existing Systems

  • Select projects by economic return, productivity, housing impact, regional benefit and resilience.
  • Keep strategic infrastructure under Australian control.
  • Maintain modern digital infrastructure for business, remote work, education, healthcare and regional development.

Abolished Or Phased Out

  • No major abolition proposed in this summary.

Why It Matters

  • Better infrastructure lowers freight, transport, housing and business costs while supporting growth.

Healthcare

Aim

  • Maintain universal access to essential healthcare while keeping the system sustainable.
  • Focus healthcare on patient outcomes rather than administrative growth.

New Measures

  • Define needs-blind essential healthcare while recognising that not every payment, medicine, lifestyle service or low-value intervention is essential.
  • Consider modest GP and specialist co-payments with exemptions for vulnerable Australians.
  • Strengthen fraud controls against over-servicing, provider exploitation and administrative abuse.

Changes To Existing Systems

  • Improve Medicare sustainability during the transition.
  • Reform public hospitals around efficiency, waiting times and outcomes.
  • Reduce duplication between Commonwealth and state programs.
  • Link workforce planning to education and migration.

Abolished Or Phased Out

  • Phase down low-value, loosely defined or non-essential publicly funded services where they do not meet essential-care standards.

Why It Matters

  • Healthcare stays universal where it is essential, but within boundaries future taxpayers can sustain.

Welfare And NDIS

Aim

  • Protect people in genuine hardship while encouraging independence and participation where possible.
  • Keep disability support focused on severe, permanent and genuine need.

New Measures

  • Separate emergency support, the essential safety net and long-term non-emergency welfare.
  • Apply citizen-first and contribution-first rules for most non-essential welfare.
  • Strengthen NDIS provider accountability, price discipline, fraud prevention and participant outcome controls.

Changes To Existing Systems

  • Keep emergency and essential services available where life, safety, child protection, disaster response or severe disability require support.
  • Use welfare pathways into employment, training, apprenticeships, education, housing stability and independence.
  • Reset NDIS boundaries around genuine clinical, functional and care needs.

Abolished Or Phased Out

  • Phase out loosely defined NDIS supports, waste, rorts, leakage and provider exploitation.
  • Restrict long-term non-emergency welfare for non-citizens and people without sufficient contribution, subject to essential-service exceptions.

Why It Matters

  • NDIS assumptions need validation. Welfare reform supports the model by keeping the safety net humane, trusted and affordable.

Government Reform

Aim

  • Make government smaller, more effective, more accountable and easier to use.
  • Reduce bureaucracy without weakening essential functions.

New Measures

  • Target a long-term 20% Australian Public Service staffing reduction through attrition, automation, consolidation and process improvement.
  • Introduce 10-year sunset reviews for major programs, agencies and regulations.
  • Publish clearer progress reports, savings, milestones, risks and performance outcomes.

Changes To Existing Systems

  • Consolidate overlapping departments and clarify federal-state responsibilities.
  • Reduce consultant spending through better internal capability, procurement controls and program simplification.
  • Use digital government and outcome-based budgeting to improve service delivery.

Abolished Or Phased Out

  • Phase out programs, grants, agencies and regulations that cannot justify their cost, complexity or national benefit.

Why It Matters

  • Government efficiency assumptions need validation. A simpler state lowers compliance costs and frees resources for tax relief, services and the Future Generations Fund.

Justice

Aim

  • Protect law-abiding citizens, uphold equal citizenship and maintain confidence in the rule of law.
  • Reject two-tier justice, political favour and identity-based treatment.

New Measures

  • Apply the hierarchy: deterrence, community protection, restitution, punishment and rehabilitation.
  • Strengthen victim recognition, restitution and communication.
  • Strengthen fraud enforcement and anti-corruption penalties.

Changes To Existing Systems

  • Improve court efficiency, case processing, administration and access to justice.
  • Give police the resources needed to protect communities and maintain public order.
  • Keep rehabilitation where appropriate, but not at the expense of community safety.

Abolished Or Phased Out

  • Remove unequal treatment before the law, including institutional shielding or identity-based exceptions.

Why It Matters

  • Justice protects public trust, property rights, investment confidence and community safety.

Defence

Aim

  • Preserve sovereignty, deter aggression and make Australia resilient during international instability.
  • Build capability through preparedness, not symbolic announcements.

New Measures

  • Maintain physical reserves of fuel, medicines, essential supplies and critical industrial inputs.
  • Expand sovereign defence industry where practical, including shipbuilding, ammunition, advanced manufacturing, defence technology and logistics support.
  • Support voluntary national service opportunities in emergency response, disaster relief, civil defence and community resilience.

Changes To Existing Systems

  • Maintain current defence spending in the initial reform phase, with future increases considered as finances strengthen.
  • Prioritise maritime security, northern Australia preparedness, cyber resilience and value-for-money procurement.
  • Keep alliances strong while preserving independent sovereign decision-making.

Abolished Or Phased Out

  • No major abolition proposed in this summary.

Why It Matters

  • Defence protects the security and resilience needed for prosperity to last.

Foreign Affairs

Aim

  • Advance Australian sovereignty, prosperity, security and stable international relationships.
  • Engage with the world while keeping independent decision-making.

New Measures

  • Use an Australia-first foreign policy based on national interest, sovereignty, prosperity, security and long-term stability.
  • Diversify trade and key dependencies.
  • Strengthen supply-chain security and strategic industry capability.

Changes To Existing Systems

  • Maintain open trade where practical, economic diplomacy, regional engagement and strong alliances.
  • Treat China as both a major trading partner and strategic competitor, using constructive engagement and strategic caution.
  • Keep strategic assets such as ports, energy infrastructure, telecommunications and critical infrastructure under Australian control.

Abolished Or Phased Out

  • Phase out unnecessary dependency in critical supply chains and strategic assets.

Why It Matters

  • Foreign affairs protects the external conditions needed for trade, investment, energy security and long-term national wealth.

How The Cards Fit Together

  • Tax reform simplifies incentives.
  • Fiscal discipline protects the budget.
  • Resource reform captures fair value.
  • The Future Generations Fund preserves national wealth.
  • Housing, energy, infrastructure, education and healthcare lower pressure and build capability.
  • Welfare, government reform, justice, defence and foreign affairs protect trust, resilience and sustainability.
