The Policy Pillars That Support The Reform Model

APP means the Australian Prosperity Program - a reform model and policy development project.

APP is not a launched political party and this material is not a final election-costed platform.

The Reform Model page explains how the full system works together. This page explains the policy pillars that support that model.

Each pillar needs further development, costing, legal review and public testing before it could become a final platform.

Tax Reform

Tax Reform simplifies the system, broadens the base and rewards work, saving, enterprise and investment.

It supports the reform model by making the revenue system easier to understand, harder to game and less dependent on repeatedly increasing pressure on current income.

Fiscal Framework

The Fiscal Framework sets rules for spending discipline, transparency and long-term budget responsibility.

It supports the reform model by making sure tax reform and resource reform are not consumed by uncontrolled program growth.

Future Generations Fund

The Future Generations Fund preserves and invests a defined share of national wealth for future Australians.

It supports the reform model by turning finite or temporary gains into permanent assets rather than short-term spending.

The $75B figure is a mature base-case contribution target after full ramp-up and validation, not a Year 1 amount.

Resource Wealth Reform

Resource Wealth Reform seeks fair public value from finite resources, beginning with the current gas-focused numerical model.

It supports the reform model by creating a disciplined revenue stream that can help build the Future Generations Fund.

Gas revenue assumptions need validation, and non-gas resources are not yet numerically modelled.

Housing

Housing policy should increase supply, improve security and reduce structural pressure on households.

It supports the reform model by attacking one of the largest cost-of-living pressures rather than relying only on temporary relief payments.

Energy

Energy policy should protect affordability, reliability, domestic capacity, industrial capability and national resilience.

It supports the reform model by reducing household and business cost pressure while strengthening Australia's productive base.

Infrastructure

Infrastructure policy should focus on projects that lift productivity, reduce bottlenecks and support national capability.

It supports the reform model by lowering hidden costs across the economy and improving the return on public investment.

Welfare And NDIS

Welfare and NDIS reform should protect people in genuine need while setting clear boundaries, integrity rules and sustainable program design.

It supports the reform model by keeping the safety net humane without allowing essential support systems to become fiscally uncontrolled.

NDIS assumptions need validation before they should be treated as firm savings numbers.

Healthcare

Healthcare policy should preserve access to essential care while improving system discipline, prevention, workforce capacity and value for money.

It supports the reform model by protecting a core service within clear fiscal limits.

Education

Education policy should build skills, literacy, discipline, opportunity and national capability.

It supports the reform model by strengthening the human capital needed for long-term productivity and prosperity.

Government Reform

Government Reform should simplify administration, reduce duplication, improve accountability and focus the public sector on core functions.

It supports the reform model by lowering compliance burdens and improving confidence that public money is being used well.

Government efficiency assumptions need validation.

Justice

Justice policy should uphold law and order, equal citizenship, due process and community safety.

It supports the reform model by protecting the rule-of-law foundation required for trust, investment and social stability.

Defence

Defence policy should protect sovereignty, deterrence, readiness and national resilience.

It supports the reform model by recognising national security as a first-principles function of government.

Foreign Affairs

Foreign Affairs policy should advance Australian sovereignty, trade, regional stability and practical alliances.

It supports the reform model by protecting the external conditions needed for prosperity and national resilience.

Family, Population And Migration

Family, Population and Migration policy should balance social cohesion, workforce needs, housing capacity, infrastructure capacity and national identity.

It supports the reform model by linking population settings to real service, housing and infrastructure capacity rather than treating growth as automatic.

How The Pillars Fit Together

These pillars support the reform model, but they do not replace it.

The full economic model belongs on the Reform Model and 25-Year Plan pages.

Policy Foundations should remain a plain-English map of the policy areas that need to carry the model in practice.
