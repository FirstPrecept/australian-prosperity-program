Policy Foundations

The doctrine and principles behind the reform model.

The Reform Philosophy

APP means the Australian Prosperity Program — a reform model and policy development project.

The Australian Prosperity Program (APP) is built on national wealth creation.

It asks government to do more than collect taxes and spend them each year.

It asks government to protect essential services, reward productive effort, control spending and build permanent national assets.

Civic Purpose

Many Australians are disengaged from politics and governance because they do not see a serious long-term plan.

They see more of the same: higher costs, more complexity, more taxes, more pressure and no clear end point.

The Australian Prosperity Program tries to set a national vision and shared purpose: stewarding national wealth, rebuilding prosperity, lowering structural cost pressures and creating a better future.

There should be an end in sight: an end to the current tax-and-cost spiral, an end to permanent short-term crisis management, an end to treating Australians as an unlimited revenue source, and a pathway toward lower future tax pressure, stronger services, national wealth and a better future.

What Government Should Exist To Do

Government should exist to do the things citizens cannot do alone:

  • protect the nation;
  • uphold law and order;
  • build and maintain infrastructure;
  • provide shared services;
  • steward national wealth;
  • protect basic rights and equal citizenship;
  • provide essential services and a genuine safety net;
  • build long-term national capability.
Government should be a trusted steward serving all Australians — protecting citizens, building shared prosperity, and never treating people as subjects to manage or resources to mine.

What Government Should Not Become

Government should not be about:

  • social engineering;
  • controlling every part of people's lives;
  • treating citizens as subjects;
  • endless bureaucracy;
  • using Australians as an unlimited revenue source.

National Wealth Creation

  • better fiscal discipline;
  • stronger productive capacity;
  • fair resource returns;
  • long-term investment;
  • less future dependence on taxation.

Intergenerational Stewardship

Australia is an inheritance.

Each generation should leave the country stronger, wealthier and more resilient than it found it.

Public debt, weak institutions, unfunded promises and consumed resource wealth all shift burdens onto future Australians.

Norway Proves The Principle. APP Adapts The Model.

Norway shows that finite resource wealth can be converted into long-term national wealth.

APP does not propose a simple copy of Norway.

Australia has different resources, federal arrangements, royalty systems, private investment structures and fiscal pressures.

Finite resource wealth should be captured fairly, protected from short-term political consumption and invested for the future.

Equal Citizenship

Equal citizenship means same rules, same standing, same treatment and same basic services.

It does not mean equal incomes. It does not mean equal outcomes. It does not mean unlimited taxpayer-funded benefits.

Needs-Blind Essential Services

Basic services should be provided according to need, not wealth or status.

This includes emergency care, essential healthcare and core education.

But not every payment, medicine, service or support is essential. Essential services need clear boundaries.

Safety Net, Not Lifestyle

The safety net should protect Australians in genuine hardship.

It should be humane and strong.

But it should not become a permanent lifestyle system or a tool for endless redistribution.

Aspiration And Reward

Australia should reward people who work, save, build skills, start businesses, take risks and improve their lives.

The system should not punish effort or success.

Simplicity And Reset

Government should be simple enough for ordinary people to understand.

Simpler systems cost less, cause less stress, are harder to game and are easier to manage.

Structural Cost-Of-Living Relief

Cost-of-living relief should come from structural reform, not temporary handouts alone.

  • tax pressure;
  • housing costs;
  • energy costs;
  • infrastructure bottlenecks;
  • government complexity;
  • long-term dependence on annual taxation.

Energy Sovereignty

Australia should not be an energy-rich country with energy-poor households and businesses.

Energy policy should protect affordability, reliability, domestic capacity, critical supply chains, industrial capability and national resilience.

Future Generations Fund

The Future Generations Fund is the long-term institution that converts reform gains and resource wealth into permanent national assets.

It is not a short-term spending account.

It should be protected by capital protection rules, high-threshold withdrawal rules, independent reporting, conservative modelling and parliamentary accountability.
