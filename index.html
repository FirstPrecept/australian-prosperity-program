A Reform Model For National Wealth Creation

Australia needs more than another round of temporary spending promises.

APP means the Australian Prosperity Program - a reform model and policy development project.

The Australian Prosperity Program is a discussion model for rebuilding national prosperity through tax simplicity, spending discipline, resource wealth stewardship and long-term investment.

The basic idea is simple:

  • reset the tax system;
  • control spending growth;
  • capture fair value from finite national resources;
  • build a Future Generations Fund, a protected national investment fund designed to turn temporary reform gains and resource wealth into permanent national assets;
  • lower structural cost pressures over time;
  • protect essential services;
  • reward work, enterprise, saving and responsibility;
  • leave future Australians with stronger national assets.

This is not a promotional brochure. It is a reform model for serious review.

Why This Matters

Many Australians are disengaged from politics and governance because they do not see a serious long-term plan.

They see more of the same: higher costs, more complexity, more taxes, more pressure and no clear end point.

This reform model attempts to set a national vision and shared purpose. It gives Australians a goal to work toward: stewarding national wealth, rebuilding prosperity, lowering structural cost pressures and creating a better future.

There should be an end in sight:

  • an end to the current tax-and-cost spiral;
  • an end to permanent short-term crisis management;
  • an end to treating Australians as an unlimited revenue source;
  • a pathway toward lower future tax pressure, stronger services, national wealth and a better future.

What Government Is For

Government should exist to do the things citizens cannot do alone:

  • protect the nation;
  • uphold law and order;
  • build and maintain infrastructure;
  • provide shared services;
  • steward national wealth;
  • protect basic rights and equal citizenship;
  • provide essential services and a genuine safety net;
  • build long-term national capability.
Government should be a trusted steward serving all Australians - protecting citizens, building shared prosperity, and never treating people as subjects to manage or resources to mine.

Government should not be about:

  • social engineering;
  • controlling every part of people's lives;
  • treating citizens as subjects;
  • endless bureaucracy;
  • using Australians as an unlimited revenue source.

The Core Proposition

Australia currently relies heavily on taxing current workers and businesses, then distributing the money through annual budgets.

That model is under pressure.

It faces:

  • an ageing population;
  • rising healthcare and disability costs;
  • housing pressure;
  • energy insecurity;
  • weak budget discipline;
  • growing dependence on complex tax and welfare systems;
  • limited conversion of finite resource wealth into permanent national assets.
What if Australia built permanent national wealth, then used that wealth to reduce future tax pressure and strengthen national resilience?

The tax model is built around equal rates, simple rules and fewer workarounds. It reduces the deduction maze, removes hidden cost taxes and gives people more control over the money they earn.

The model does not rely only on government payments to relieve pressure. It seeks to remove taxes that help drive up everyday costs, so households and businesses keep more of their own money in the first place.

Some taxes pass through the economy and compound. Stamp duty increases the cost of housing. Payroll tax increases business costs that are recovered through goods and services. Fuel excise increases transport costs, and transport sits underneath almost every supply chain. Removing embedded taxes lowers the cost base instead of collecting money first and redistributing it later.

Key Features

Tax Simplicity

A simpler and more equal tax system with a $40,000 tax-free threshold, a flat 25% personal income tax rate above the threshold, 25% company tax, broad 20% GST, no negative gearing, no CGT discount and simplified deductions. People who earn more still pay more in total dollars, but the state does not take a higher proportion of each additional dollar.

Cost of Living Relief

Structural reform aimed at reducing pressure through tax, housing, energy, infrastructure and simpler government.

Inflationary Tax Removal

Removing or phasing down taxes embedded in prices and transactions, including stamp duty, payroll tax, fuel excise, luxury car tax and selected excises, so Australians keep more money and have more choice.

Spending Discipline

Clearer fiscal rules, controlled spending growth and a safety net focused on genuine need.

Future Generations Stewardship

Resource Wealth Reform and a Future Generations Fund to convert part of finite resource value into permanent national assets.

The Long-Term Target

The current base case uses a mature Future Generations Fund contribution target of $75B per year once the full reform model is operating.

This is not a Year 1 assumption.

Early contributions may be modest. Contributions rise as the fiscal reset, tax transition, resource revenue reforms and spending controls mature.

Under the current base case, the Future Generations Fund reaches approximately $4.7437T by Year 25.

$4.7437T is the current base-case Year 25 model output within the broader $3T-$5T long-term target range.

After Year 25, a prudent 3% drawdown rule would provide approximately $142B per year, subject to capital protection, conservative modelling and independent oversight.

Why Resource Wealth Matters

Australia has finite natural resources. Those resources should not disappear into annual budgets alone.

A defined share of resource wealth should become permanent national assets for future Australians.

Norway proves the principle. APP adapts the model.

Australia is not Norway. Australia has different resources, federal arrangements, private investment structures, royalties, population needs and fiscal pressures. But the lesson remains useful: finite resource wealth can be captured fairly, protected from short-term political consumption and invested for the future.

Important Caveats

Model caveats The current numerical Resource Wealth Reform model is gas-focused only. Non-gas resources are not yet numerically modelled. NDIS sustainability and boundary reform savings, government efficiency savings and gas revenue assumptions require further validation. The model is staged and timing-aware, but it is not yet a fully ramped annual cashflow model.
