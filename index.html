The Problem

Australia is caught in a tax-and-cost spiral.

Working citizens are treated as the easiest resource to tax. Government keeps adding complexity, hidden taxes, market distortion and bureaucracy, while finite national resources are not being converted into enough permanent national wealth.

Short election cycles encourage temporary fixes instead of generational planning.

The Core Proposition

The Australian Prosperity Program asks a different question:

What if Australia built permanent national wealth, used its resources properly, simplified tax, reduced government-driven cost pressures and governed for the next generation instead of the next election?

The model aims to move Australia from managed decline to generational stewardship.

The Future This Model Points Toward

The long-term goal is a country with lower tax pressure, stronger public finances, more disciplined government, better infrastructure, greater national resilience and a permanent national wealth base.

The current model uses a Future Generations Fund to turn reform gains and resource wealth into long-term national assets. Over time, that national wealth is intended to reduce pressure on workers, households and businesses.

The ultimate aspiration is much lower taxation over the long run, potentially moving toward a 10% tax environment only after national wealth, spending discipline and Fund maturity make it credible.

This is not a Year 1 promise. It is the long-term direction the model is designed to test.

What We Are About

1. Smaller, Focused And Efficient Government

Government should do fewer things, do them better, and cost less doing them. Its proper role is infrastructure, essential services, public safety, border integrity, national defence, shared systems and long-term stewardship.

2. No Social Engineering, Market Manipulation Or Government-Caused Inflation

Government should stop using taxes, subsidies, regulation and bureaucracy to distort markets, manipulate behaviour or drive up the cost of living. Public policy should reduce the cost base, not inflate it.

3. Reward Work, Aspiration And Responsibility

Australia should reward people who work, save, build, start businesses, raise families and take responsibility. The tax system should be simpler, flatter and fairer, not a punishment for effort and productivity.

4. Equality, Not Equity

Citizens should be equal before the state. Government services should be needs-blind. Safety nets should protect citizens from genuine hardship, but the state should not sort Australians into identity groups, preferred classes or permanent dependency categories.

5. Australians First, National Wealth And Generational Stewardship

The first duty of the Australian Government is to Australian citizens. Australia should stop treating working citizens as the easiest resource to tax while giving away national resources cheaply. Rocks are resources, not people. The goal is wealth creation and stewardship, not wealth redistribution and managed decline.
